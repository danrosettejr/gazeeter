Cebu City

The Queen City of the South

Cebu City is a first-class highly urbanized city in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. As the country's oldest city, it is a significant center of commerce, trade, and education in the Visayas. Known as the **"Queen City of the South,"** Cebu is a vibrant metropolis that harmoniously blends its rich historical and colonial past with modern economic development and natural beauty.

Economy & Landmarks

Cebu's economy is one of the largest and most dynamic in the Philippines, driven by information technology, business process outsourcing (BPO), tourism, shipping, and furniture-making. The city is a major tourist destination due to its historical sites and proximity to world-class beaches.

  • Magellan's Cross: A Christian cross planted by Portuguese and Spanish explorers as ordered by Ferdinand Magellan.
  • Basilica Minore del Santo Niño: The oldest Roman Catholic church in the country, founded in 1565.
  • Fort San Pedro: A military defense structure, built by the Spanish.
  • Taoist Temple: A colorful temple with a towering, multi-tiered design located in the Beverly Hills subdivision.

History

Cebu is the cradle of Christianity in the Far East. Before the arrival of the Spanish, it was already a prosperous settlement with trade links to other parts of Asia. On April 7, 1521, Ferdinand Magellan landed in Cebu. It became the first Spanish settlement and capital of the Philippines, a distinction it held until the capital was moved to Manila in 1571.

Geography & Climate

The city is located on the central eastern coast of Cebu Island. Its terrain is characterized by a coastal plain that gradually rises to rolling hills and mountains. Cebu City has a tropical monsoon climate, with a lengthy wet season and a short dry season.
