Davao City

The Crown Jewel of Mindanao

Davao City is a highly urbanized city on the island of Mindanao, Philippines. It is the largest city in the Philippines in terms of land area and serves as the main trade, commerce, and industry hub of Mindanao. Known as the **"Durian Capital of the Philippines,"** Davao is a melting pot of cultures, offering a unique blend of urban sophistication and natural wonders, from pristine beaches to the nation's highest peak, Mount Apo.

Economy & Landmarks

Davao's economy is primarily driven by agriculture and agribusiness, particularly in the production of durian, bananas, and cacao. The city is also a growing hub for manufacturing, real estate, and tourism. It is known for its safety, cleanliness, and numerous natural and man-made attractions.

  • Mount Apo: The highest mountain in the Philippines, a popular destination for hikers.
  • Philippine Eagle Center: A conservation breeding facility for the critically endangered Philippine eagle.
  • Samal Island: A nearby island famous for its white-sand beaches and resorts.
  • Roxas Avenue Night Market: A bustling market for street food and bargain shopping.

History

The area of Davao was originally inhabited by various indigenous groups. The modern city was founded by Don Jose Oyanguren in 1848 after defeating local Moro leaders. Significant development occurred during the American colonial period with the establishment of large-scale plantations. Davao became a chartered city in 1937 and has since grown into the most populous city in Mindanao.

Geography & Climate

Davao City's landscape is diverse, encompassing flat plains, rolling hills, and the mountainous terrain of Mount Apo. It is located on the shore of the Davao Gulf. The city enjoys a tropical rainforest climate, with relatively uniform temperature, humidity, and rainfall throughout the year. It is situated outside the typhoon belt.
